Who am I and what do I do?

Since the age of six, video games have been my passion. Although my first encounter with games was on the Atari 2600, my first gaming platforms were the NES, SNES, and a Celeron 300 PC. Throughout my childhood, I observed the gaming industry's development, which rapidly accelerated from a niche hobby to a globally popular form of entertainment, rivaling and, in some cases, even surpassing the film industry. A good example of this is the range of emotions games can evoke, especially the ability to make players feel guilt (e.g., in RPGs, where decisions impact NPC characters and further storyline development), something previously unattainable in the film narrative.

My fascination with games and my way of thinking - I have always been interested in how different mechanisms work, how something is built, how a specific effect was achieved - eventually evolved into a desire to develop skills in creating computer games. In 2021, I graduated from the University of Economics and Computer Science in Krakow, majoring in Computer Science and Econometrics at specialization of "designing and producing computer games" and have successfully defended my engineering thesis titled "Design and Production of a 2D Computer Game based on the student project 'Swords and Jokes' ". I recently completed internship preparing for a position as a Unity Developer "Intern at Code Element company". My next goal is to become a valuable member of a team creating innovative and immersive games, so that I can combine my passion with my professional career and continue to expand my knowledge and skills.

To this day I am an active gamer, currently playing on the PS5 console, Android mobile devices, and PC. I have the ability to easily connect with others and work in a team, and I am open to new challenges and eager to continue my development. Becoming a valuable member of a team creating innovative and immersive games is a natural goal for me, as it allows me to combine my passions with my professional career.

— Released Project/Post-Launch Support

Micro Car Drive

Micro Car Drive is an arcade mobile game that has been released on the Google Play Store. It is currently in the support and development phase post-launch. It is a two-person project, created in collaboration with a friendly graphic designer. I am the main project ideator and responsible for all the mechanics available in the game and, in general, the entire 'programming' layer of the game. I was also in charge of most of the prototypes, designs, and inspirations for the final models executed by the other person in Blender. Additionally, I handled proper work organization and project management. The game is available as a free production with implemented advertising and microtransactions. After the launch, we added integrations with Google Play Game Services, allowing players to utilize cloud save/load progress system. Thanks to the support of players after the release, we appropriately adjusted the difficulty level and eliminated major bugs. We are continuously working on the game's marketing and its development, currently focusing on introducing the best lap times and online rankings.

— Completed Project/GROUP PROJECT (4-PERSON TEAM)

Swords & Jokes

This project was a student endeavor, developed by a four-person team during the final semesters of our studies. Based on this project, I pursued my engineering thesis titled "Designing and Producing a 2D Computer Game based on the student project Swords and Jokes".

The game is a 2D action RPG with a comedic and parody twist. Our main objective was to create a functioning game with NPC characters, a combat system featuring melee, ranged, and spell attacks, as well as mana and HP systems, and a Dash mechanic for acceleration. A crucial aspect was that all in-game graphics were entirely crafted by our team members. Two individuals served as programmers, one was the main graphic designer, and the last person was responsible for organizing the project and creating the levels. I took on the role of the lead programmer and team leader. Despite having specific roles assigned, each team member was flexible and often performed tasks beyond their designated roles. For instance, I, as the lead programmer, also designed the main UI, some levels, certain particle effects, and modified VFX. I crafted the death screen, contributed significantly to the game's storyline, and wrote all the dialogues found in the game (simple dialogues), among other tasks.

— COMPLETED PROJECT

Hedgehog Shooter

This is a 3D arcade game project inspired by the game "Archero," designed specifically for Android devices. I developed the game during several months of internship as a Unity Developer at Code Element company. The graphics in the project are purely for demonstration purposes, as the main goal was to enhance my programming skills by creating diverse mechanics and solving encountered challenges. Throughout the internship, I also learned to utilize GIT version control through the command line, allowing me to work on different "branchach" efficiently, creating pull requests, creating "commits", removing unnecessary changes and working with Azure DevOps.

The game features three difficulty modes, challenging the player to complete all levels with the best possible time. There is also a "Survive" mode, where the player faces an increasing number of enemies spawning in waves, with the next wave appearing five seconds after eliminating the last enemy from the previous wave.

Speed Ball

The project was initiated right after I completed my studies and was developed by a two-person team. The target platform is Android mobile devices. In the project, I was responsible for all mechanics, controls, and everything related to programming and the Unity engine. I was also the main visionary behind the project.
